## Cover Letter

Dear Editors,

We submit "SIMSIV: A Calibrated Agent-Based Framework for Studying Gene-Culture Coevolution in Pre-State Societies" for consideration at JASSS. The paper presents a calibrated agent-based model of band-level human societies featuring 35 heritable traits with empirical heritability coefficients, 9 interacting simulation engines, and automated calibration against 9 ethnographic benchmarks using simulated annealing (816 experiments, perfect score achieved).

We believe JASSS is the appropriate venue because the paper's primary contribution is methodological: a validated ABM platform for testing gene-culture coevolution hypotheses. The ODD protocol description, sensitivity analysis, and held-out validation (mean score 0.934 across 20 independent runs) follow JASSS standards for reproducible computational social science. The simulation code is open-source and fully reproducible from a single random seed.

The central empirical finding — that institutional governance produces immediate behavioral effects (49% violence reduction, 40% inequality reduction) while heritable cooperation traits show no detectable divergence across governance regimes at 500 years — contributes directly to the debate between co-evolutionary (Bowles & Gintis, 2011) and substitution (North, 1990; Waring & Wood, 2021) models of gene-culture interaction. We state this finding at appropriate confidence given the single-band limitation and 500-year timescale.

Sincerely,
The Author
